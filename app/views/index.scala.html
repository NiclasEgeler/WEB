Welcome to Minesweeper!

How to Play

Minesweeper is a classic single-player puzzle game. The objective is to clear the playing area without detonating any mines. Here's how to play:

  • Click on any square to start the game.
  • If the clicked square is not a mine, it will reveal a number indicating how many mines are adjacent to it.
  • If the clicked square is a mine, you lose the game.
  • Use the numbers as clues to determine the location of mines and click on squares you think are safe. If a square has no adjacent mines, it will automatically expand to reveal surrounding squares.
  • Your goal is to clear all non-mine squares without clicking on any mines. You can mark suspected mine locations with flags to help keep track.

    • History

    Minesweeper has a rich history dating back to the 1960s. It gained worldwide popularity when it was included in the Microsoft Windows operating system in the early 1990s. The game was created by Curt Johnson and was inspired by a mainframe computer game called "Relentless" developed by Robert Donner in the early 1980s.

    Over the years, Minesweeper has become an iconic puzzle game and is loved by players of all ages.
